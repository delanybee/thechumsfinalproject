About This Analysis

Overview

This dashboard tracks the global coverage of economic policies designed to regulate carbon emissions. These policies include carbon taxes, cap-and-trade systems, and carbon credit or offset mechanisms.

Policies may operate at the regional, national, or multinational level. Regional policies, such as those implemented by states or cities, are included in the statistics of their respective countries. For multinational coalitions, such as the European Union, each participating country receives weighted policy credit based on its share of GDP.

Timeline & Metrics

The analysis spans 35 years, from 1990 to 2025, allowing users to explore how the coverage of economic climate policies has evolved over time.

Alongside the timeline, a tracker displays the share of global carbon emissions currently covered by economic policies.

Selecting a country reveals its contribution to total global emissions as well as the extent to which its emissions are covered by economic policy mechanisms.

Data Sources

Carbon emissions data were sourced from Our World in Data, and information on economic policy coverage was drawn from the World Bank's Carbon Pricing Dashboard.
